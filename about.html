Key Facts About Indonesia’s Maritime Boundaries

Understanding Indonesia’s ocean sovereignty through law, history, and geography.

Territorial Sea

Indonesia’s territorial sea extends 12 nautical miles from its baselines, granting full sovereignty similar to land territory.

Read More

Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ)

The EEZ covers 200 nautical miles from Indonesia’s baselines, giving rights to explore, exploit, conserve, and manage natural resources.

Read More

Continental Shelf

Indonesia has sovereign rights over the continental shelf, which may extend beyond 200 nautical miles where geological evidence supports it.

Read More

Archipelagic State Principles

The Djuanda Declaration (1957) established Indonesia as an archipelagic state, later recognized in UNCLOS 1982 and ratified through Law No. 17/1985.
