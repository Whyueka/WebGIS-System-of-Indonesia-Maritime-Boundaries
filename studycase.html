Study Case

Case Studies on Indonesia’s Maritime Boundaries

Indonesia–Australia

All maritime boundaries settled through treaties (1971, 1972, 1997, 1998). Strong cooperation in fisheries and maritime security.

Indonesia–Malaysia

Territorial sea mostly agreed, but Ambalat Block remains disputed. Ongoing negotiations with potential ICJ involvement.

Indonesia–Timor Leste

Land boundary mostly settled. Maritime delimitation in progress (latest talks 2019–2022).

Indonesia–Philippines

EEZ boundary agreement signed in 2014. Cooperation on fisheries and patrols, though minor technical adjustments remain.

Indonesia–Papua New Guinea

Land boundary defined, maritime delimitation progressing. Joint border committee active, but EEZ boundary still under review.

Indonesia–Vietnam

Indonesia and Vietnam reached an agreement on their EEZ boundary in the North Natuna Sea, reflecting commitment to UNCLOS 1982 and regional stability.
